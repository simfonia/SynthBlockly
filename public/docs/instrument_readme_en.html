Synthesizer Types Description

In the "Create Instrument" block, you can choose from four different synthesis methods:

1. PolySynth (Standard)

Method: Standard Subtractive Synthesis.

Characteristics: Pure and intuitive sound. The "bread and butter" of the synth world.

Best for: Basic electric pianos, simple leads, and bass lines. Perfect for learning how ADSR works.

2. AMSynth (Amplitude Modulation)

Method: Uses one oscillator to rapidly modulate the volume (amplitude) of another.

Characteristics: Has a distinct "shimmering" or "vibrating" quality, often resembling a fast-moving organ.

Best for: Retro electronic effects and unique organ-like timbres.

3. FMSynth (Frequency Modulation)

Method: Uses one oscillator to rapidly modulate the pitch (frequency) of another.

Characteristics: Complex, bright, and often carries a strong digital or metallic character.

Best for: Bells, metallic percussion, and sharp digital bass. This is the iconic sound of 80s pop music.

4. DuoSynth

Method: Layers two independent synthesizer voices to create a thick, rich texture.

Characteristics: Very thick, powerful, and layered sound.

Best for: Grand pads, powerful synth brass, or aggressive lead sounds.

Subtractive vs. Additive Synthesis

These are the two most fundamental approaches to sound design, representing opposing philosophies: Sculpting vs. Stacking.

1. Subtractive Synthesis - Like "Sculpting"

The most common method, used by instruments like PolySynth.

  • Core Logic: Start with more, end with less. Begin with a harmonically rich waveform (like a Sawtooth) and use a Filter to cut away unwanted frequencies.
  • Analogy: Like chipping away at a block of marble to reveal a statue.
  • Sound: Warm, powerful, classic. Ideal for Bass, Leads, and Pads.

2. Additive Synthesis - Like "Building Blocks"

Used by Custom Waveform Instruments (Harmonic/Free Frequency).

  • Core Logic: Start with less, end with more. Begin with silence and stack multiple pure Sine Waves on top of each other.
  • Analogy: Like building a shape with Lego bricks, or a pointillist painting made of many dots.
  • Sound: Bright, metallic, glassy. Ideal for Bells, Organs, and Sci-Fi effects.
