Step Sequencer Guide

The Step Sequencer is a fundamental tool for electronic music. It divides one measure into 16 equal slices (16th notes), allowing you to create complex rhythms and arpeggios with simple strings.

1. Parameters

  • Sound Source: Specify the instrument (e.g., Piano, Bass) or drum keyword (KICK, SNARE, HH, CLAP).
  • Use Chord Names?: When checked, identifiers in the sequence string are treated as "Defined Chord Names". When unchecked, they are treated as "Single Note Names".
  • Measure N: Specifies which bar this block controls. Supports variables or math operations for dynamic scheduling.
  • Sequence String: Defines the rhythmic content.

2. Syntax Symbols

SymbolDescription
xTrigger default sound (drums or C4 note).
C4, G3...Specific single note name (useful for Bass or melodies).
Cmaj7, Dm7...(Chord Mode) Specific defined chord name.
. or -Rest (silence).
| or SpaceVisual separators.

3. The 16-Step Rule

The system always parses one measure as 16 steps. We recommend using | to separate every 4 steps:

// Example: 12-bar Blues sequence with "Use Chord Names" enabled C7 . . . | . . C7 . | . . . . | . . C7 .

4. Pro Tips

  • Loops Only: This block must be placed inside an "Every N measure loop" to function.
  • Metronome Benefit: If your track uses heavy samplers like Piano, add a "Count In" block at the start. This gives the browser time to load assets before the first bar begins.
