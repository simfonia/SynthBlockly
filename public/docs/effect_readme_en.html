Effect Block Detailed Guide

General Parameters

  • Wet: Controls the dry/wet mix of the effect (0 to 1). A value of 0 means no effect is audible (dry), while 1 means only the effect's output is heard (wet).

Distortion

Adds harmonic grit and saturation to the sound.

  • Amount: The intensity of the distortion, ranging from 0 (clean) to 1 (heavily distorted).
  • Oversample: Reduces aliasing, which can cause unwanted high-frequency noise. Using '2x' or '4x' results in a smoother, higher-quality sound at the cost of slightly more processing power.

Reverb

Simulates the sound of a space (a room, hall, etc.).

  • Decay: The length of the reverb tail in seconds. Longer decay times simulate larger spaces.
  • Predelay: The time in seconds between the original sound and the start of the reverb reflections. A short predelay can help maintain the clarity of the original sound.

Feedback Delay

Creates echoes of the input sound.

  • Delay Time: The time between successive echoes. This can be a musical duration (like '8n' for an eighth note) or a time in seconds.
  • Feedback: The strength of the echoes, from 0 to 1. A value of 0.25 means each echo will be 25% of the previous one's volume. High values can lead to self-oscillation.

Filter

Shapes the tonal characteristics of the sound by boosting or cutting specific frequencies.

  • Type: The kind of filter to apply (e.g., lowpass lets low frequencies through, highpass lets high frequencies through).
  • Frequency: The cutoff or center frequency of the filter in Hertz (Hz).
  • Q (Resonance): The width of the filter band. For lowpass/highpass filters, a higher Q value creates a resonant peak at the cutoff frequency. For bandpass filters, it narrows the band.
  • Rolloff: The steepness of the filter cutoff in decibels per octave. Steeper values (e.g., -24, -48) create a more aggressive filtering effect.

Compressor

Reduces the dynamic range of a sound, making quiet parts louder and loud parts quieter.

  • Threshold: The audio level in decibels (dB) above which compression starts. The range is approximately -100 to 0. A lower value (e.g., -50) means more of the signal will be compressed.
  • Ratio: The amount of compression applied. For example, a ratio of 4 means for every 4 dB the input signal goes above the threshold, the output signal will only increase by 1 dB.
  • Attack: The time in seconds it takes for the compressor to start reducing the gain once the signal exceeds the threshold.
  • Release: The time in seconds it takes for the compressor to stop reducing the gain after the signal falls below the threshold.

Limiter

A type of extreme compressor that prevents a signal from exceeding a set threshold.

  • Threshold: The level in decibels (dB) that the signal is not allowed to exceed. The maximum value is 0. The range is approximately -100 to 0.
